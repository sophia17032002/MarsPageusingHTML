Humans live on Mars
is it possible?


Living on Mars is theoretically possible but involves overcoming significant challenges such as its thin, mostly carbon dioxide atmosphere, extreme temperatures, high radiation levels, and low gravity. Essential solutions include developing reliable life support systems, creating sustainable habitats using Martian resources, and ensuring a degree of self-sufficiency for food and water. While current technology and research, along with international collaboration, are paving the way, human colonization of Mars remains a goal that could potentially be realized within the next few decades.

  1. 01

    USA started the exploration of Mars

    The United States, through NASA, initiated Mars exploration with the Mariner 4 mission, which launched in 1964 and performed the first successful flyby of Mars in 1965.

  2. 02

    Soviet Union/Russia

    The Soviet Union (now Russia) also made early attempts to explore Mars with its Mars program, starting with Mars 1 in 1962, although their early missions faced various challenges and failures.

  3. 03

    European Space Agency (ESA)

    Europe's ExoMars programme has always had the ambition to marry exploration with science. ESA's vision is to send Europeans to Mars by 2040. .

  4. 04

    China (CNSA)

    China has more recently entered Mars exploration, with its Tianwen-1 mission, which successfully placed an orbiter around Mars and landed the Zhurong rover on the Martian surface in 2021.

  5. 05

    India (ISRO)

    ISRO uses a method of travel called a Hohmann Transfer Orbit – or a Minimum Energy Transfer Orbit – to send a spacecraft from Earth to Mars with the least amount of fuel possible.

  6. 06

    United Arab Emirates (UAE)

    On 9 February 2021, the United Arab Emirates became the first Arab country and the fifth country to reach Mars and the second country to successfully enter Mars' orbit on its first try after India.
